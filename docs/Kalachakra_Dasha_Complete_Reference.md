## 1. FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT

Kalachakra Dasha = "Wheel of Time" Dasha. Parasara called it **"the most respectable of all dasa systems"** (BPHS 46.6-11). Before explaining it to Maitreya, Parasara prayed to the Lord — a gesture not enacted for any other dasha.

**Key distinction from Vimshottari:**
- Vimshottari = planetary dasha (based on Moon's nakshatra)
- Kalachakra = **rashi (sign) dasha** (based on Moon's nakshatra PADA / navamsha)
- Vimshottari shows the state of the native's **mind** as time progresses
- Kalachakra shows the **physical and spiritual experience** — the "how" and "where"

**Birth time sensitivity:** 1 minute error in birth time shifts all Kalachakra dasha dates by ~3 months. This makes it both extremely difficult to use with unverified times AND the premier tool for birth time rectification.

## 3. THE TWO CHAKRAS: SAVYA AND APASAVYA

### 3.1 Savya Chakra (Direct/Zodiacal)
The 12 navamsha signs in order: **Ar, Ta, Ge, Cn, Le, Vi, Li, Sc, Sg, Cp, Aq, Pi**

Planetary lordship sequence: Mars, Venus, Mercury, Moon, Sun, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Saturn, Jupiter

### 3.2 Apasavya Chakra (Irregular/Anti-zodiacal — "Mirror Image")
The 12 navamsha signs: **Sc, Li, Vi, Cn, Le, Ge, Ta, Ar, Pi, Aq, Cp, Sg**

The Apasavya Chakra is formed by the **mirror images** of zodiacal signs:
- Ar ↔ Sc (both Mars)
- Ta ↔ Li (both Venus)
- Ge ↔ Vi (both Mercury)
- Cn ↔ Cn (Moon owns only Cancer — mirrors itself)
- Le ↔ Le (Sun owns only Leo — mirrors itself)
- Sg ↔ Pi (both Jupiter)
- Cp ↔ Aq (both Saturn)

**Why the jumps?** The lordship constraint forces irregular progression. Starting from Scorpio going anti-zodiacally: Sc(Mars), Li(Venus), Vi(Mercury), then Moon and Sun must come — but they own only Cn and Le — so it jumps to Cn, Le, then back to Ge(Mercury), Ta(Venus), Ar(Mars), Pi(Jupiter), Aq(Saturn), Cp(Saturn), Sg(Jupiter).

## 6. THE STRUCTURAL LOGIC (PVR Narasimha Rao's Discovery)

### 6.1 The Z and M(Z) Pattern

Let Z = zodiacal sequence: Ar, Ta, Ge, Cn, Le, Vi, Li, Sc, Sg, Cp, Aq, Pi
Let M(Z) = mirror images: Sc, Li, Vi, Cn, Le, Ge, Ta, Ar, Pi, Aq, Cp, Sg

Write Z and M(Z) consecutively = 24 signs. Write this cycle 3 times = 72 signs.
Split into dasha cycles of 9 signs each = 8 dasha cycles.

These 8 cycles correspond to the 4 padas × 2 groups within the Savya mega-cycle.

Similarly, inverse of Z = Pi, Aq, Cp, Sg, Sc, Li, Vi, Le, Cn, Ge, Ta, Ar and its mirror image, written 3 times and split into 8 cycles of 9, gives the Apasavya mega-cycle.

### 6.2 The Nava-Navamsha (D-81) Insight

**PVR Narasimha Rao's critical discovery:** The "dasha sequences" listed in BPHS for each pada are actually the **nava-navamsha (D-81)** — navamsha within navamsha — signs corresponding to the 9 equal parts of each pada.

**Derivation example — Ashwini 1st Pada:**
- Ashwini 1st pada = Aries navamsha
- Treat Aries as a rashi and find navamshas within it
- Aries contains: Ashwini 1, 2, 3, 4 + Bharani 1, 2, 3, 4 + Krittika 1
- Kalachakra navamshas: Ar, Ta, Ge, Cn, Le, Vi, Li, Sc, Sg
- This matches the dasha sequence exactly!

**Derivation example — Ashwini 2nd Pada:**
- Ashwini 2nd pada = Taurus navamsha
- Taurus contains: Krittika 2, 3, 4 + Rohini 1, 2, 3, 4 + Mrigashira 1, 2
- Kalachakra navamshas: Cp, Aq, Pi, Sc, Li, Vi, Cn, Le, Ge
- Note: Krittika is Savya, Rohini/Mrigashira are Apasavya — uses Kalachakra navamsha (not standard navamsha)

### 6.3 The Mahadasha vs. Bhukti Controversy

**Standard view (most scholars):** The 9-sign sequences are Mahadashas.

**Raghavacharya's view (supported by PVR):** The 9-sign sequences are actually **Antardashas/Bhuktis**, not Mahadashas. Mahadashas are a progression of Moon's navamsha (like Vimshottari is progression of Moon's nakshatra).

**Key verse (often ignored by scholars):**
> *"narasya janma kaale vaa prasna kaale yadamsakah*
> *tadaadi nava raaseenaamabdaastasyaayuruchyate"*

Translation: "Whatever navamsha is active at birth or prasna, years of nine amshas starting from it form the ayu (longevity)."

**Raghavacharya method (Mrigashira 4th pada example):**
- Mrigashira 4 = Aries Kalachakra navamsha
- 9 Mahadashas = 9 consecutive padas from Mrigashira 4: Mrigashira 4, Ardra 1, 2, 3, 4, Punarvasu 1, 2, 3, 4
- Their Kalachakra navamshas: Ar, Pi, Aq, Cp, Sg, Ar, Ta, Ge, Cn
- The BPHS sequences (Sg, Sc, Li, Vi, Le, Cn, Ge, Ta, Ar) are used for **Antardashas within** each Mahadasha

## 8. THE THREE GATIS (Jumps in the Wheel)

### 8.1 Simhavalokana Gati (Lion's Leap)
**Definition:** Trinal jump — 5th or 9th sign jump
**Occurrences:** Sg→Ar or Ar→Sg; Pi→Sc or Sc→Pi

**Results in Savya:** Fear of animals, loss of friends, distress to near relations, fall in dungeons, danger from poison and weapons, fall from a vehicle, fever, destruction of house

**Results in Apasavya:** Death of father or elders, loss of position

### 8.2 Markati Gati (Monkey's Leap)
**Definition:** Temporary reversal of direction
**Occurrences in Savya:** Cn→Le (going as Sc, Li, Vi, **Cn, Le**, Ge...)
**Occurrences in Apasavya:** Le→Cn (going as Ar, Ta, Ge, **Le, Cn**, Vi...)

**Results in Savya:** Loss of wealth, agriculture and animals, death of father or elders

**Results in Apasavya:** Danger from water, distress to father, loss of position, anger of rulers, wandering in the forests

### 8.3 Mandooki Gati (Frog's Leap)
**Definition:** Skipping one sign
**Occurrences in Savya:** Vi→Cn (skipping Le) and Le→Ge (skipping Cn)
**Occurrences in Apasavya:** Ge→Le (skipping Cn) and Cn→Vi (skipping Le)

**Results in Savya:** Distress to relatives, elders and father; trouble from poison, weapons, enemies, thieves. Specifically in Le→Ge leap: death of mother or native, trouble from rulers and diseases.

**Results in Apasavya:** Distress to wife, loss of children, fever, sickness and loss of position

### 8.4 Directional Implications (BPHS)
- Vi→Cn leap: East gives great results
- Le→Ge leap: Do NOT travel East; South-West may benefit
- Sg→Ar leap: North and East are fruitful; avoid South and West
- Pi→Sc leap: Specific directional results vary by chart

## 9. DEHA AND JEEVA RASHIS

### 9.1 Definition
- **Deha Rashi:** Physical body, health, material manifestations, external environment
- **Jeeva Rashi:** Consciousness, vital life-force, mental well-being, spiritual evolution

### 9.2 Determination Rules
- **Savya nakshatras:** Deha = 1st dasha sign; Jeeva = 9th dasha sign
- **Apasavya nakshatras:** Deha = 9th dasha sign; Jeeva = 1st dasha sign (REVERSED)

### 9.3 Interpretation
- Malefic (Sa, Ma, Ra, Ke) transiting Deha → physical injury, chronic illness, accidents
- Malefic transiting Jeeva → severe mental anguish, threat to longevity
- Benefic (Ju, Ve, Me) transiting Deha → physical vitality, material prosperity
- Benefic transiting Jeeva → positive spirit, cheerfulness, energy

**Note:** Deha/Jeeva hold for one born at the BEGINNING of the pada. For births deeper into the pada, the effective Deha/Jeeva may shift.

## 10. BALANCE OF DASHA CALCULATION

### 10.1 Formula
Each Pada = 3°20' = 200 minutes of arc.

Let L = Moon's longitude, S = end longitude of current Navamsha (Pada).

**Minutes remaining = (S - L) converted to minutes of arc**

**Balance fraction = Minutes remaining / 200**

**Balance of first dasha sign = Balance fraction × Duration of starting sign**

### 10.2 Alternative (Full Paramayus method)
Balance of entire cycle = Balance fraction × Paramayus of the 9-sign cycle

Then subtract completed dasha durations from the beginning to find the running dasha and its remaining period.

### 10.3 Worked Example (from BPHS Santhanam)
Moon at 3°Cn 00' (Cancer), born June 1, 1950.
- Moon is in 4th pada of Punarvasu
- Punarvasu = Savya-I (Ashwini group)
- 4th pada sequence: Cn, Le, Vi, Li, Sc, Sg, Cp, Aq, Pi (Paramayus = 86)
- End of 4th pada of Punarvasu = 3°20' Cancer = 3.3333°
- Fraction remaining = (3.3333 - 3.0000) / 3.3333 = 1/10 = 0.1
- Cycle remaining = 86 × 0.1 = 8.6 years
- Dasha durations from start: Cn=21, Le=5, Vi=9... total starting from Pi backward: Pi=10, Aq=4, Cp=4... 
- Running dasha at birth = **Pisces** (with 0.6 years remaining out of 10)

## 11. CYCLE CONTINUATION (After 9 Dashas Complete)

### 11.1 The Mega-Cycle Concept
4 padas × 2 groups within each cycle (Savya or Apasavya) = 8 dasha cycles forming one mega-cycle.

**Progression order within Savya mega-cycle:**
Savya-I Pada 1 → Savya-I Pada 2 → Savya-I Pada 3 → Savya-I Pada 4 → Savya-II Pada 1 → Savya-II Pada 2 → Savya-II Pada 3 → Savya-II Pada 4 → (back to Savya-I Pada 1)

**Progression order within Apasavya mega-cycle:**
Apasavya-II Pada 1 → Apasavya-II Pada 2 → Apasavya-II Pada 3 → Apasavya-II Pada 4 → Apasavya-I Pada 1 → Apasavya-I Pada 2 → Apasavya-I Pada 3 → Apasavya-I Pada 4 → (back to Apasavya-II Pada 1)

**Note:** In Apasavya, Group II comes FIRST (before Group I).

### 11.2 Scholarly Controversies
1. **Continuous Progression** (BPHS weight): Move to next pada's sequence
2. **Same Pada Reset:** Restart at 1st sign of same pada
3. **Portion Zero (South Indian):** Each pada's cycle is standalone

## 12. ANTARDASHA COMPUTATION

Antardashas within a dasha start with the dasha sign itself and proceed cyclically through all 9 signs in the dasha cycle.

**Duration of each antardasha:**
Antardasha duration = (Mahadasha duration × Antardasha sign's dasha years) / Paramayus

## 13. INTERPRETATION FRAMEWORK

### 13.1 What Kalachakra Dasha Shows
- Physical and spiritual experience (vs. Vimshottari's mental focus)
- Longevity assessment (Ayurdaya) — its primary traditional use
- Direction of life events
- Material vs. spiritual balance through Deha-Jeeva axis

### 13.2 Using Divisional Charts (Critical — PVR's emphasis)
"Rely on divisional charts when judging Kalachakra dasha! Look for repeating factors."

- If dasha sign is 4th from Mars in D-4 AND contains 4th lord → property purchase likely
- D-10 SAV with 30+ rekhas in a sign → best career phase during that sign's dasha
- D-24 SAV with 30+ rekhas → best learning period
- D-7 analysis for children, D-9 for marriage

### 13.3 Argala (Intervention) with Kalachakra
Planets in 2nd, 4th, and 11th from a dasha sign cause argala (intervention).
Planets in 12th, 10th, and 3rd from the sign obstruct the argala.

If a sign has argala from its lord, yogakaraka, or relevant significator → matters of that sign get a boost during its dasha.

### 13.4 Transit Correlation
If planets occupying or aspecting the dasha rashi, Deha rashi, or Jeeva rashi in a divisional chart also do so in transit (in Rashi chart), important events relating to that divisional chart's significations are likely to fructify.

### 13.5 Longevity Categories (from Moon's position in Pada)

| Moon's Position | Category | Result |
|----------------|----------|--------|
| Beginning of Pada | Purna Ayu (Full) | Full lifespan, success |
| Middle of Pada | Madhya Ayu (Medium) | Moderate achievements |
| End of Pada | Alpa Ayu (Short) | Short span, suffering |
